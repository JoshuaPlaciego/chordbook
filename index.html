How to read the charts

🎸
The Fretboard Grid Horizontal lines are the guitar strings (the bottom line is your thickest E string, the top line is your thinnest e string). Vertical lines are the metal frets on the neck.
🔢
Position Numbers The numbers beneath the grid indicate exactly which fret position your hand should start at on the guitar neck.
🔵
Blue Number Bubbles These show you where to press your fingers. 'R' is the Root note (the core note of the chord). Numbers like 3 or 5 represent musical intervals (the 3rd or 5th note of the scale).
❌
Open Strings & Mutes A Green O means you should strum that string open (without pressing any fret). A Red X means you MUST NOT play or strum that string at all.
⚡
Yellow Sequence Line This dashed line visualizes the "geometric shape" of the chord across the strings, training your brain to memorize the pattern so you can slide it anywhere on the neck.

How to read the piano charts

🎹
The Keyboard Layout The graphic perfectly mirrors a real piano keyboard. The lower wide keys are the natural white keys (C, D, E, F, G, A, B), and the upper floating keys are the sharps/flats.
🔵
Highlighted Keys The brightly colored purple and indigo keys indicate the exact notes that belong in this chord voicing.
✨
Animated Key Presses The keys visually "press down" and glow yellow in a sequence. This sequential animation shows you the recommended order to arpeggiate or roll the chord.
🎼
Inversions vs Extensions Inversions take the standard chord and shift the bottom note to the top, letting you play the same chord higher or lower to fit the mix. Extensions (like Sus2) swap notes out entirely to create lush, atmospheric pad sounds.
